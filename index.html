Literature

  • books
  • literature
  • reading
  • recommendation
  • 9.2.2025 09:53:43
  • Neurocomic: A black and white comic showing everything on how the brain works. From love, to neurons, to synapses, to neurotransmitters - everything is depicted.
  • Mindware from Richard Nisbett: Is an introduction on how we think or what makes us really deciding for something. It is a lot about specific heuristics and how they influence our choice behaviour.
  • Equality from Thomas Piketty: The author tries to answer the complex issue of inequality within our society and what democracies can do about it like inheritance taxes to the mechanisms the small elite uses to concentrate their power.
  • Einsteins Spuk from Anton Zeilinger: A detailed and depicting introduction into the problems of quantum physics. Very interesting.
  • Homo Sapiens from Yuval Harari: Is also a very depicting overview into humankind's history. It explains how money and companies work together and how our society is built by using our believe for non-existing things.
  • How to win an information war from Peter Pomarantsev: A very detailed book about how the British used the system of propaganda to fight back during second world war.
  • The art of cyber warfare by Peter Kestner: A very interesting book with many historical references, like Sun Tzu, discussing all the key features of what makes someone win a war (like strategy vs tactics).
  • Making Sense of Mind Only by William Waldron: The book explains the differences and historical developments between all the buddhistic schools and explains what makes Yogacara so special. It explains how our mind is bound by our believes and how we can tackle those delusions.
  • Waking Up by Sam Harris: A compelling book about the difference between spiritually and religion, with insights about the authors life and experiences with psychedelic drugs.
  • How emotions are made by Lisa Feldman Barrett: In the book she presents her theories about emotions and health. Very insightful.
  • The age of surveillance capitalism by Shoshana Zuboff: A milestone in economic science. It analyizes how the big tech cooperations are using our data to fuel algorithms to sell advertisment and how they could destroy democracy by that.
